The basics: Income Share Agreements

An Income Share Agreement is defined as a contract between two parties, where one party provides a certain amount of financing to pay for the other party’s education. In return the other party promises, as per the contract, to pay a fixed percentage of their income – however that is defined – to the party that provided the financing.

This means there are two main elements to an income share agreement: the fixed percentage of income that is paid by the student and the duration that the student will pay this.

The percentage of income is fixed, no matter what the income is. The percentage is based on a certain expectation of the future earnings of the student, but if this is less than expected, or more than expected, the percentage will not change. That is both the risk and the upside for the investor.

The Alfie ISA: Students Meet Investors

At Alfie we have developed the ISA in such a way that it is most beneficial for students and investors: investors can invest in many students at the same time to reduce risks, while students can be completely independent from investors.

Even better, if a student ends up getting rich, we are giving them the option to buy themselves out, so that they will never have to pay more than what is reasonable.

The Alfie advantages explained

Graduate debt free

The Alfie ISA is an equity model, as opposed to a debt model. This might sound confusing, but it is really simpler than it seems: just like companies can give out stocks (equity) in exchange for a share of their future earnings, we let you give out stocks in your future income, for a fixed period of time. You will never have to worry about having debt, or paying off a debt: you just pay a fair percentage of your income, no matter what that income is, for a fixed period of time. After that time you don’t owe us anything, even if the total you paid is less than we invested in you.

This gives you a risk-free education, flexibility after graduation, and an incentive for us to help you with your career.

Some FAQs

Is there any regulation on ISAs?

Yes, currently ISAs are considered investment contracts, and therefore are governed by the SEC. We have worked closely together with the SEC and the CFPB to assure that we are offering the best product for both students and investors.

Additionally there is a new law currently introduced in congress, that would provide additional clarity. For example, it regulates the maximum duration and the maximum percentage that can be asked. We have designed our ISAs to be far below both proposed maxima, to ensure the best interest for students and investors.

Am I stuck to Alfie forever?

No! Absolutely not. The Alfie ISA is set for a fixed period of time, after which you will never have to worry about payments to Alfie again. Even better: with Alfie you can choose to buy a 'buy out'-option, that allows you to buy yourself out whenever you are making a lot more money than expected. That way you will never have to worry about paying us too much.

How much money can I get?

That depends on the institution: we only provide up to what the institution thinks you need. This includes living expenses and tuition, but not more than that. This way we ensure that you never take out more money than is financially responsible.

What is the percentage of my income that I have to pay?

That is an excellent question of which the answer depends on two different variables: how much money you need and what your prospects for your future earnings are. As this is different for every person, we provide everybody with a tailored rate. This is calculated by our proprietary algorithms, based on factors like the degree you are pursuing and the school where you are pursuing that degree. By applying through the button above we can provide you with a tailored rate. Applying is free and without consequences, so no need to hold back.
